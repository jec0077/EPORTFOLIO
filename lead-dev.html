Leadership Development

My leadership journey through the years

Youth Leading Youth

My leadership journey began at Apostolic Faith Church. At age 12, I was named Youth Co-Coordinator of the Youth Assistance Ministry. This leadership role required me to organize and plan Sunday morning youth services with youth ministers and church leaders. I was also responsible for managing audio and video technologies being used in services like PowerPoint presentations, video projectors, and audio engineering, to name a few. My team created a more efficient system preparing church leaders to teach children using technology to add a fun and engaging twist.

ENpowered to create

In the Summer of 2018, I spent my time creating robots with Project Syncere. The ENpowered Summer Program was a month-long robot construction team competition to empowered minority high school to gain interest in Computer Engineering, Computer Science, and other related fields. Each team had three roles. My role was Designer-Programmer. My job was to design the robot's code to function properly and make the robot's design unique. As the Designer-Programmer, I was the leader of the team creating and engineering most to the robots' components. My team created an excellent code and impressive robot that won 2ND PLACE of the entire competition. Summer Robotics team

Leading by example

During my high school years at Perspectives Charter Schools, I slowly developed leadership by being an example. I graduated Salutatorian of the Class of 2020 [ranked 3RD in entire class] and knew for my hard work and excellent character become a shining example of a student at Perspectives.

Leader in the workspace

Internships were a huge building block for me to grow as a professional and a leader in my field. Working with several Fortune 500 companies (GTCR), I learned professional skills that have elevated my leadership to the next level. I received awards from Genesys Works for demonstrating hard work, professionalism, and commitment to school and work. Maintaining high honors and professional growth during this time. The Genesys Works Most Valuable Professional Award.
As a part of Chicago Scholars, creating skills and relationships can help me professionally and academically.

Future Executive Leader in Student Organizations

I am also serving as the National Society of Black Engineers University of Illinois at Chicago Chapter Executive Board Treasurer. Help form a community of career-ready black STEM students at UIC and increase the membership of the NSEB-UIC chapter.
